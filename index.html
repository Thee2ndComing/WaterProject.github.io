How might we use technology to reduce the amount of water contamination?

Problem

Water in some communities is dirty and contaminated. We plan to halt day Zero by making some device that can filter out contaminants from water.

Water contamination is having very devastating effects on communities. Humans can’t live without water, and there are places in the world without proper access to it. Such as Mexico and Subsaharan Africa. If people can’t drink it, people go thirsty and die.

Or, people drink questionable water sources, and it has horrible effects on people's body. Such as Diarrhea. Most kids under the age of five die from water-related diseases such as diarrhea.

We plan to stop this by making a small cheap reusable device to prevent this from occurring as often.

Average size village to average water gallons.

Because the water management of clean water in african villages are so poor they end up using up to 185 liters per person daily in the villages. Population was anywhere between 1,000 up to 20,000 population. Which makes use of 48,871 gallons to 977420 gallons a day.

Thinking Process

We wanted to think as clean as possible. We researched ways to distill water and make it clean enough to drink. We even considered trying to make water from other elements. However, this proved to be a complicated process. Also, making a reusable bottle is a challenging design project. Glass and paper have higher reusability than plastic does. So we thought of a model that could be 3-D printed in a glass bottle, which uses two reusable objects.

Once we thought of this idea, we also had to make a target audience with a feasibility principal with the resources that we have available. We felt that we could target the Sub-Saharan Africa region or the Mexico region. They both had bodies of water near them that were salty, so if we had a type of filter that utilized all the different types of screen. It could filter out most of the contaminants. Filters are quite prominent but are usually only used on taps in America. We needed a Device that would target all types of water contamination. So we thought of a very different kind of filter.

Prototype

We had to conduct interviews and make a design sketch. We learned quite the amount of information from both.

The interviews gave us a clear understanding of how much people understand about water pollution, which was very basic. People knew that big companies were constantly polluting drinking water sources and had no qualms about it. They don't feel an emotion to the issue. However, when informed about *Day Zero, most people felt a variety of adverse reactions.

They weren’t aware of an urgent issue, yet they didn’t know that our Day Zero is decades away. The reason is because of a lack of information on their end. Along with some education with these filters, a population would work toward better water reservoirs and more excellent clean water supply.

*Day Zero- The same day an event occurs or vulnerability discovered. In this case, it's a water availability concern. Meaning there isn’t any water available in the taps, which is a very dire situation.

We called it the SPEC.

Conclusion

In solving water contamination in the minority community, we develop a water filter called the SPEC. The SPEC will purify the water by separating the harmful contaminants from the water. This prototype idea came from the interviews and from several different sources. First, we are trying to target our own minority communities. Next, we are going to expand out to places that are about to reach Day Zero.
